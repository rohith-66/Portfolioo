Bike demand forecasting

Forecasted short-term demand to support redistribution planning and reduce peak-hour availability gaps.

Business problem

Bike-sharing systems often become imbalanced: some stations overflow at peak hours while others run empty. Operations teams need short-term demand forecasts to proactively redistribute bikes and reduce service gaps.

Data and constraints

  • Historical rental data with timestamps, station identifiers, and usage counts.
  • Weather and calendar signals used as external features.
  • Messy time-series: missing values, outliers, and strong seasonality.
  • No true streaming pipeline — forecasts built from periodic snapshots.

Approach

  1. Aggregated and cleaned time-series data using SQL and Pandas.
  2. Performed EDA to identify daily/weekly seasonality and peak windows.
  3. Benchmarked baseline forecasting approaches to capture actionable signal.
  4. Packaged forecasts + station risk views into a Power BI dashboard for planners.

Key insights

  • Strong daily + weekly demand patterns; peak windows are consistent.
  • Weather materially affects short-term demand (rain reduces trips).
  • A small subset of stations repeatedly experience shortages during peak windows.
  • Simple forecasting baselines captured most of the actionable signal needed for redistribution.

Outcome

Delivered a forecasting workflow and dashboard that highlighted high-risk stations and time windows. This supported redistribution planning and helped reduce peak-hour availability issues.

What I’d do next

  • Integrate near-real-time telemetry for rolling forecasts and live rebalancing.
  • Build station-level optimization to suggest redistribution routes.
  • Set up monitoring for forecast accuracy and drift to maintain reliability over time.
