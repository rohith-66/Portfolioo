Bike Demand Forecasting

Short-term demand forecasting to support bike redistribution planning and reduce peak-hour availability gaps.

Problem

Bike-sharing systems become imbalanced: some stations overflow at peak hours while others run empty. Operations teams need short-term demand forecasts to proactively redistribute bikes and reduce service gaps.

Approach

  • Prepared time-series rentals using SQL + Pandas (aggregation, missing values, outliers, seasonality checks).
  • Analyzed daily/weekly seasonality and peak windows to identify actionable station-time risk patterns.
  • Benchmarked forecasting baselines to capture the strongest operational signal with minimal complexity.
  • Delivered forecasts and “high-risk station” views in a planner-friendly Power BI dashboard.

Insights

  • Stable peak windows: daily and weekly peaks repeat reliably, making redistribution timing predictable.
  • Weather matters: rain and temperature shifts materially change short-term demand.
  • Risk concentrates: a small subset of stations repeatedly drive the majority of peak-hour shortages.
  • Simple wins: baseline forecasting captured most actionable signal needed for ops planning.

Outcome

  • Delivered a forecasting workflow + dashboard highlighting high-risk stations and high-risk time windows.
  • Enabled planners to allocate redistribution effort where it prevents the most service gaps.

Next steps

  • Integrate near-real-time telemetry for rolling forecasts and live rebalancing.
  • Build station-level optimization to recommend redistribution routes.
  • Set up monitoring for forecast accuracy and drift.
